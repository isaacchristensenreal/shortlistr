What is ATS?

The software that decides if a human ever sees your resume

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software that companies use to filter job applications before any human reviews them. When you apply online, your resume goes directly into the ATS — not into a recruiter's inbox.

The system parses your resume into plain text, scans it for specific keywords from the job description, and assigns a match score. If your score falls below the company's threshold — often 60–75% — your resume is automatically moved to a rejection folder. The recruiter never opens it.

Over 99% of Fortune 500 companies and most mid-size companies use ATS software on every application they receive. If you've been sending applications and hearing nothing back, the ATS is almost certainly why.

Why resumes get rejected

The 6 reasons your resume never reaches a human

None of these are about your qualifications. They're all about how your resume is read by software.

🔑
Wrong keywords

Your resume uses different words than the job description — even if they mean the same thing. "Led a team" ≠ "cross-functional leadership" to an ATS parser.

📐
Two-column layout

ATS reads horizontally across both columns simultaneously, producing garbled text. Your Python experience gets merged with a bullet about team leadership. Both score zero.

🎨
Design tool export

Canva, InDesign, and Figma embed text as image layers. The ATS receives a PDF with no extractable text. Your entire resume is blank to the system.

📁
Contact info in header

Word and Docs document headers — the same region used for page numbers — are skipped by most ATS parsers. Your name and email are invisible to the system.

📋
Generic resume

Sending the same resume to every job means your keyword match score is low for all of them. Every job description is different. Your resume needs to reflect each one.

🔤
Unusual section headers

"Where I've Made an Impact" fails. ATS expects "Work Experience." Nonstandard headers cause your experience to be misfiled — or ignored entirely.
