What actually works

The 3 Changes That Move the Needle Most

These are ranked by impact. Do them in order. The first one alone typically increases ATS scores by 20–35 points.

1

Mirror the job description's exact vocabulary

Replace your generic resume language with the exact phrases from the job description. "Led cross-functional teams" instead of "managed multiple groups." "Data-driven decision making" instead of "used data to guide choices." The underlying experience is identical — the ATS score is not.

⚡ +20 to +35 ATS points
2

Put keywords in experience bullets, not just a skills list

ATS weights keywords found in your work experience section more heavily than a standalone skills list. "Python" in a skills row scores lower than "Built data pipelines in Python" in an experience bullet. Rewrite 3–5 bullets to include your highest-priority keywords in context.

⚡ +10 to +18 ATS points
3

Remove tables, columns, and graphics

Multi-column layouts, text boxes, icons, and graphics cause ATS parsers to jumble or skip your content entirely. A visually polished resume that the ATS can't read scores zero on keywords — regardless of what's written. Convert to single-column plain text structure.

⚡ +10 to +25 ATS points (if formatting was broken)

Do all three before you apply to anything. Combined, these changes move most resumes from the 30–45% range into the 70–85% range — from auto-rejected to in front of a recruiter.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Why am I applying to jobs but not getting interviews?
    The most common reason is ATS filtering. Most companies use software that automatically scores and rejects resumes before any human reads them. If your keyword match score is below ~60%, your application is typically auto-rejected. This is why you can apply to 50 jobs and hear back from 2 — it's not your qualifications, it's your ATS score.
  • What is a good interview rate for job applications?
    A healthy interview rate is typically 10–20% — meaning 1–2 interviews for every 10 applications. Most job seekers who aren't tailoring their resume to ATS see rates of 2–5% or lower. After fixing ATS keyword alignment, interview rates typically jump to 15–25% on targeted applications.
  • Does applying to more jobs increase your chance of getting an interview?
    Not if the underlying problem is ATS filtering. Sending the same resume to 100 jobs produces roughly the same low interview rate as sending it to 20. The fix is improving your ATS score on each application, not increasing volume. Fewer targeted, keyword-optimized applications consistently outperform high-volume generic ones.
  • How do I get more callbacks from job applications?
    The three highest-impact changes are: (1) mirror the exact keywords from each job description in your experience bullets, (2) move skills keywords into context in your experience section rather than just listing them, and (3) fix formatting issues like tables and columns that prevent ATS from reading your resume.
