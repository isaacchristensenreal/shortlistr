The Three Resume Problems

What's Actually Stopping Your Search

Every resume problem falls into one of three buckets. Each one has a different fix — and they're not equally likely to be hurting you.

📐

Format Problems

Your resume's structure breaks ATS parsing. The system misreads or skips entire sections — so even great content never gets scored.

  • Two-column or multi-column layout
  • Tables used for skills or experience
  • Creative templates from design sites
  • Text in headers, footers, or text boxes
  • Saved as an image or scanned PDF
🔑

Keyword Problems

Your resume doesn't use the same language as the job description. ATS matches exact phrases — different words for the same skill = no match.

  • Applied to 10+ jobs with zero callbacks
  • Never tailoring resume for each application
  • Using outdated or informal terms for tools
  • Missing abbreviations or acronyms from JD
  • No Skills section with hard skills listed
✍️

Content Problems

Your resume passes ATS but doesn't impress the human reader. Weak bullet points, no numbers, or a story that doesn't match the role.

  • Getting interviews but not advancing
  • Bullets that list tasks, not achievements
  • No quantifiable results or metrics
  • Generic summary or objective statement
  • Experience that doesn't clearly map to the role

ATS & Your Resume

How ATS Affects Every Problem Type

Applicant Tracking Systems are involved at every layer of the resume problem — not just keywords. Here's how ATS intersects with all three problem types.

Format

ATS Parsing Failure

If your format is broken, ATS can't extract your content correctly. It may score your resume at 0% — not because your keywords are missing, but because it can't read them. This is the most silent killer.

High risk · silent failure
Keywords

ATS Keyword Scoring

ATS compares your resume text against the job description word by word. Each missing phrase lowers your score. Most systems set a threshold — usually around 60–70% — below which resumes don't reach a recruiter.

High risk · most common
Content

ATS Has Lower Stakes Here

Content quality — the strength of your bullet points and the story your resume tells — matters mostly to human readers. ATS doesn't evaluate impact or nuance. Fix format and keywords first, then polish content for the humans who see it.

Lower risk from ATS
