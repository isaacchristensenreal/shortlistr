How ATS Reads Technical Skills Differently

General ATS keyword matching is already unforgiving. For technical roles, it's worse — because the tech vocabulary has hundreds of acceptable abbreviations, version numbers, and aliases that ATS may or may not normalize.

🔤

Abbreviation vs. full name

Many ATS platforms treat abbreviations and full names as separate tokens. A JD saying "Kubernetes" may not match "k8s" — and vice versa. The safe move is always listing both.

JD: "Kubernetes"
Resume: "k8s" → miss on many ATS
Fix: "Kubernetes (k8s)" → both matched
☁️

Cloud provider vs. specific services

"AWS" on your resume doesn't match "EC2", "Lambda", or "S3" in the JD. ATS treats the service names as separate keywords. List the specific services you've used, not just the provider.

JD: "EC2", "Lambda", "S3"
Resume: "AWS" → 0 of 3 matched
Fix: "AWS (EC2, Lambda, S3)" → 4 matched
🗄️

Shortened database names

"Postgres" is universally understood by humans. ATS isn't human. "PostgreSQL" is the official name that appears in JDs — and many ATS platforms don't normalize the short form to the full name.

JD: "PostgreSQL"
Resume: "Postgres" → miss on strict ATS
Fix: "PostgreSQL" → always matched
📐

Framework vs. language confusion

"React" is not the same keyword as "JavaScript." "FastAPI" is not the same as "Python." ATS scores each term independently — naming just the framework without the language (or vice versa) can leave easy matches on the table.

JD: "JavaScript", "React"
Resume: "React" only → 1 of 2 matched
Fix: "JavaScript (React, Next.js)" → 2+ matched
⚙️

Version numbers as separate tokens

Some ATS platforms treat "Python 3.11" as different from "Python." Others don't. The conservative approach: list the language cleanly ("Python") first, then version in parentheses if relevant — don't lead with the version.

JD: "Python"
Resume: "Python 3.11" → inconsistent match
Fix: "Python" (3.11) → clean match first
📝

Methodology keywords get skipped

Engineers often omit soft-technical keywords like "system design," "code review," "technical documentation," and "on-call." These appear in JDs and ATS checks for them — but tech resumes rarely include them.

JD: "system design", "code review"
Typical resume: neither present
Fix: add to experience bullets naturally

The GitHub and Portfolio Problem

The most common piece of advice given to engineers — "put your GitHub on your resume" — is correct for human readers and completely useless for ATS. Here's what happens to every link you include.

⛔

ATS parses text, not URLs. When it encounters github.com/yourname, it sees a string — not a profile, not repos, not your commit history, not your tech stack. Every technology, project, and skill demonstrated in your portfolio is invisible to ATS unless you describe it in plain text on the resume itself.

The fix is simple but requires discipline: keep the GitHub link for the human who eventually reads your resume — but don't rely on it for ATS. Every technology in your repos needs to appear in plain text in your resume bullets, your Skills section, or your project descriptions. Treat your resume as the only document ATS will ever see, because it is.
